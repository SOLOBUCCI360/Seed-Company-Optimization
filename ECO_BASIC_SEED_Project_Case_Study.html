Project Case Study: Streamlining Inventory and Order Management at ECO BASIC SEED, Kaduna, Nigeria

Problem Statement

ECO BASIC SEED, a large-scale agricultural company based in Kaduna, Nigeria, faced two major challenges:

  • Inefficient Data and Inventory Management: Multiple departments were using Excel, which led to redundant data entry and confusion. There was no unified platform for real-time access to inventory and order data.
  • Discrepancies in Customer Order Forecasting: Customers made large orders (Eg 1 Ton of seeds) months in advance, but when they came to collect, they only bought a fraction (Eg 10Kg) of their order. This caused the farm to overproduce, leading to financial losses.

Objective

The goal was to implement an integrated system to:

  • Streamline Inventory Management: Centralize data input across departments to improve accuracy and accessibility.
  • Optimize Customer Order Forecasting: Ensure better alignment between customer forecasts and actual purchases, reducing overproduction and loss.

Approach/Process

  • Assessment: Evaluated existing Excel-based processes and identified inefficiencies in data management and order forecasting at ECO BASIC SEED.
  • Google Sheets and Forms Implementation: Replaced Excel with Google Sheets for live, real-time updates and Google Forms for standardized data entry across departments.
  • Centralized Database: Aggregated all data into one central sheet, ensuring real-time visibility for the warehouse, commercial, and production teams.
  • Payment-Based Order System: Introduced a payment model where customers had to pay upfront for a portion of their order. For instance if you need 3 tons of seed you paid in advance for 2 tons, if you later wanted more than 2 tons you add the required cost for that, but the customer never ordered less than they paid for after this system, ensuring commitment and reducing the risk of unsold products.
  • Testing and Rollout: Conducted training sessions and tested the system before a full rollout, ensuring smooth adoption across departments.

Challenges Faced

  • Resistance to Change: Employees were initially hesitant to switch from Excel to Google Sheets.
    Solution: Conducted training sessions and highlighted the efficiency and accuracy of real-time data.
  • Data Entry Inconsistencies: Some employees continued entering data manually instead of using Google Forms.
    Solution: Introduced monitoring processes and regular check-ins to ensure consistency.
  • Integration of Departmental Data: Different departments had their own systems for tracking data.
    Solution: Created customized templates for each department that fed into a unified database.
  • Customer Order Forecasting: Predicting demand was difficult due to order changes.
    Solution: Introduced a payment-based system that tied production to committed payments.
  • System Scalability: As operations grew, high data volume slowed down performance.
    Solution: Implemented data validation and automated reporting for smoother operations.

Results/Impact

  • Improved Data Accuracy: Real-time data entry reduced errors across departments.
  • Financial Savings: The new payment system minimized overproduction and reduced losses.
  • Better Demand Forecasting: Production was more closely aligned with actual customer needs.
  • Operational Efficiency: Teams accessed and shared data quickly, improving workflow.
  • Scalability: The system accommodated future growth with minimal technical overhead.
